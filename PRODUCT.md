# Hitch — PRODUCT.md

register: product (the app UI); the pre-read explainer page is brand register.

## Name

**Hitch.** One word carrying the whole product: *get hitched* (marry), *without a hitch* (the promise: it goes smoothly), and *hitch together* (it binds the shared record). The tagline is the value prop verbatim: "Your wedding, without a hitch." The assistant is also called Hitch, you *ask Hitch*. Renamed from the working title "Wedding Copilot" on 2026-07-02 ("copilot" is the most exhausted suffix in AI products and undersold a product that holds the record rather than riding shotgun). Mark: two interlocking rings (the couple, linked) with an over/under weave (bound, not merely overlapping).

## Product purpose

A wedding-planning platform whose assistant is grounded in the couple's real, multi-party wedding record and, on explicit approval, writes back to it. Built for a timed product exercise; designed to stand alone as an open-source demonstration of governed agent writeback. The thesis: generic AI advises; only the system-of-record can act on THIS wedding and keep its decisions consistent.

## Users

- **The couple** (primary, free tier): ~6 months out, overwhelmed, planning at 10pm after work. They need the 2am "what am I forgetting" killed, one shared truth with partner and planner, and momentum. They do not need more options; they need relief and honest counsel.
- **The wedding planner** (secondary, paid tier): runs 8-15 weddings at once, lives in her inbox. Needs every wedding's state trustworthy without chasing it, and an audit trail of what the copilot and couple changed.
- **The pre-read reader** (technical product people): evaluating the builder's judgment. The artifact must show thinking, not decoration.

## The planner agent (the differentiator)

Not a chatbot, not a cheerleader. A **steady counsel** persona, unique in a space full of sycophantic planning bots:

1. **Non-sycophantic.** It never says "great idea!" It states what the record shows, what a change breaks, and what it recommends, with reasons.
2. **Decision-realistic.** It distinguishes three classes honestly: decisions it can execute (mark done, draft the email), decisions it can frame but not make (cut the guest list? it shows the budget math and the cascade, then hands the call back), and decisions that aren't its business (who sits with your mother).
3. **Therapy-adjacent steadiness.** Weddings are a stress event wrapped in logistics. The agent's register is the calm planner at the table: acknowledges the weight ("this is the part most couples find hardest"), then returns to what is actually decided, pending, and at risk. Grounding as emotional regulation, not just data hygiene.
4. **Multiplayer honesty.** A wedding has no single owner. The agent works the seam: couple actions write to the record the planner audits; high-stakes changes name who else they affect (the caterer's headcount, the planner's timeline). It never pretends the couple plans alone.

## Tone

Warm, direct, specific. Reads like a senior planner who has done 200 weddings, not a mascot. No exclamation marks from the agent. Names and dates from the record in every substantive sentence.

## Anti-references (what this must never resemble)

- Generic SaaS AI: purple gradients, sparkle emoji ✨, "Ask me anything!"
- Wedding-industry saccharine: blush pink everything, script fonts, confetti.
- Chat-tab-bolted-on: the copilot lives inside the planning surface or it isn't the product.
- Sycophantic assistants: validation theater, hedge-everything answers, false cheer.

## Strategic principles

1. The moat is the governed multi-party record, not the model. The model is swappable; the write path is the product.
2. No write without approval; high-stakes writes name their downstream cascade first.
3. Every agent action leaves an artifact (audit), a decision (with reasoning), and a confidence read (what's reversible, what's grounded).
4. Free couple tier is the wedge; planner dependence on the record is the revenue.
5. Cede generic inspiration to ChatGPT on purpose.
