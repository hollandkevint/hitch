One page, one bet

The spec is deliberately one page: a 300-word cap forces one bet instead of a feature list. The other two thirds of the spec are the running prototype and the eval set.

Problem. Usage is dropping: couples and planners get better planning help from ChatGPT than from our three static features (guest list, seating, to-dos). We store the wedding; we don’t move it forward. We’re the system-of-record nobody opens — and the planner pays for it.

Insight. ChatGPT plans well in general but can’t see this wedding: who’s confirmed, what’s left, which vendors are booked. Today that state lives in the planner’s head, and a planner doesn’t scale. The moat is the couple’s real, multi-party state: data and workflow only we hold, not the model.

Solution (v1). A copilot inside the to-do/timeline view. It answers “what’s left?” against the real wedding, drafts the next action (the vendor email, the RSVP nudge), and on approval writes it back to the record the planner’s business runs on: task done, timeline updated, vendor logged. One high-frequency job, owned end to end — an active surface, not a chat tab.

Why now. It turns a dead system-of-record into a weekly reason to return; defensible: ChatGPT can’t act on data it can’t see.

Success. Primary: weekly active planning sessions per couple. Counter-metric: writebacks the user reverses or edits (trust, keep low). v1 target: 40% of to-do updates start in the copilot. Kill test: reversals above ~15% after two iterations — pull writeback, keep grounded answers.

Assumptions. Interviews generalize (users leave for the thinking help, not better records); planners will approve writes, not delegate them.

Out of scope (v1). Generic inspiration (cede to ChatGPT); seating and guest copilots (v2); white-label planner packaging (v2); any action without explicit approval.

Team. Design: Hitch in context, not a bot. Eng: retrieval + writeback + an eval gate on wrong actions. PMM: sell the seam — to couples, the assistant that actually knows your wedding; to planners, capacity without headcount.

How to read it

The bet is the seam: the couple’s re-engagement is the wedge, the planner’s capacity is the business. Everything the copilot writes lands in the record the planner runs their business on — see governance for how writes are gated, and decisions for what was traded away.

Proof in demo The record, writeback, and audit trail the PRD depends on. Assumptions The risky bets, first signals, and ideas held out of v1. Roadmap The v2 offering preview and read-only agent trace.
