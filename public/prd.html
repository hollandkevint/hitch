One page, one bet

The PRD is deliberately capped below 300 words: a complete, readable product bet with problem, evidence, solution, success, QA/evals, assumptions, scope, and team asks. The running prototype and process page carry the rest of the proof.

Problem. Usage is dropping: couples and planners get better planning help from ChatGPT than from our static guest list, seating, and to-do tools. We store the wedding; we don’t move it forward. Planner pays for a system of record nobody opens.

Evidence. Interviews point to thinking work leaving the product: “what’s next?”, vendor follow-up, RSVP anxiety. ChatGPT can advise generally, but it cannot see this wedding: who confirmed, what is late, which vendors are booked, or what a change cascades to. A planner’s memory does not scale.

Solution (v1). Put a copilot inside to-do/timeline. It answers “what’s left?” against the live record, drafts the next action, and, after approval, writes back: task done, timeline updated, vendor logged. One high-frequency job end to end, not a chat tab.

Success. Primary: weekly active planning sessions per couple. Input: 40% of to-do updates start in copilot. Counter-metric: reversed/edited writebacks; kill writeback if reversals stay above 15% after two iterations.

QA/evals. Seven binary evals gate release: grounded tasks, correct vendor/date, declared writes only, server-side approval on high-stakes changes, ungroundable handoff, no false-cheer judgment writes, injection safety.

Assumptions. Planner is buyer; couples are the wedge; users will approve visible writes; record accuracy is good enough.

Out of scope. Generic inspiration; seating/guest copilots; white-label planner packaging.

Team. Design: in-context action cards. Eng: retrieval, writeback, eval gate. PMM: “the assistant that actually knows your wedding” plus planner capacity without headcount.

How to read it

The product bet is a B2B2C data product: couples create the usage wedge, planners buy the capacity gain, and the agent only earns autonomy by passing the QA/eval contract.

Proof in demo The record, writeback, and audit trail the PRD depends on. Assumptions The risky bets, first signals, and ideas held out of v1. Architecture The governed record, approval gate, and data-product contract.
